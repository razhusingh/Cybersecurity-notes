# 💼 Interview Q&A
1. What are the three pillars of a SOC?

People, Process and Technology.

2. What is the primary goal of a SOC?

Continuous Detection and Response to cyber threats.

3. What is the responsibility of a SOC Analyst L1?

Monitor alerts, perform triage, validate alerts and escalate when necessary.

4. What does a SOC Analyst L2 do?

Performs deeper investigations and correlates data from multiple sources.

5. What does a SOC Analyst L3 do?

Threat hunting, advanced investigations and incident response support.

6. What is the role of a Security Engineer?

Deploys, configures and maintains security solutions.

7. What is the role of a Detection Engineer?

Creates and improves detection rules for security tools.

8. What are the 5 Ws used during alert triage?

What, When, Where, Who and Why.

9. What should a good incident report contain?

5 Ws, investigation findings and supporting evidence/screenshots.

10. What is SIEM?

A platform that collects logs, correlates events and generates security alerts.

11. What is EDR?

An endpoint security solution providing visibility, detection and response.

12. What is the difference between SIEM and EDR?

SIEM monitors logs from multiple sources, while EDR focuses on endpoint detection and response.

13. What is the role of a Firewall?

Monitors and filters incoming and outgoing network traffic.

14. What is an MSSP?

A Managed Security Service Provider that delivers outsourced SOC services.

16. Why are humans considered the weakest attack vector?

Because attackers exploit human psychology using social engineering.

# 🛡️ SOC Analyst Mindset

Whenever an alert arrives, think:
- What happened?
- Who is affected?
- Which system is affected?
- Is this malicious?
- Is there enough evidence?
- What logs should I check?
- Should this be escalated?
